# MildMate UX/UI Knowledge Base: Duvet Category Page Architecture

## 1. UX/UI Deep-Dive & Friction Analysis (Category Context)

### Desktop View: Overcoming the "Sea of Sameness" Grid
* **The Friction:** Standard home textile category pages display rows of uniform, folded products, forcing users to click through multiple tabs just to understand the functional differences between fabrics. This kills the classic web F-Pattern readability, as the eye scans horizontal listings and bounces due to information deficits.
* **The Rectification:** Lay out the category page with a sticky, left-side Technical Filter Rail matched with wide, content-rich category horizontal row banners. The first viewport must guide the user across the hero statement, down to the fabric selectors, and directly into high-ticket functional categories (e.g., Standard Home Beds vs. Bespoke Marine/RV shapes).

### Mobile View: Thumb-Driven Intent Stacking
* **The Friction:** Standard two-column product grids look exactly like low-cost marketplace apps. On a compact screen, small thumbnails hide the luxury details of our signature U-zip closures and specific material weaves, causing users to scroll endlessly without taking action.
* **The Rectification:** Implement a Single-Column Feature-Stack with swipable sub-category chips at the absolute top of the screen. Every product preview layout must feature structural micro-badges detailing key engineering advantages (e.g., `3-Sided Zipper Track`, `BreezePlus Weave`) directly inside the thumb-interaction zone.

## 2. Structural Layout Optimizations

### 🛑 Sections to REMOVE
* **Infinite Generic Product Rows:** Do not present more than 6 product variations before introducing an interactive educational block. Infinite scrolling without precise structural sorting filtering causes high drop-offs on high-AOV store profiles.
* **Stand-Alone Price Displays:** Remove naked price tags (`$195.00`) on grid previews without accompanying custom benefit copy. Naked pricing forces comparison to mass-market Shopee/Lazada imports instead of emphasizing our custom-fit engineering value.

###  Sections to ADD
* **Etsy-to-Web "Sizing Anxiety Relief" Block:** Right between product rows 1 and 2, insert an educational trust section targeted at custom sizing:
  > *"Tired of standard sizes that don't fit? 87% of our buyers switched from marketplaces because they needed an exact fit for non-standard beds, RV bunks, or yacht hulls. Select 'Custom Size' on any cover, and our design team will provide an architectural blueprint check before production."*
* **A Fabric Selection Matrix Slider:** A compact interactive slider showcasing the selection matrix, helping shoppers choose between **BreezePlus** (Hot Sleepers/Pet Owners), **CloudSoft** (Moisture Resistance), **PremaCotton** (Sensitive Skin), and **EcoLuxe** (Chemical-Free/Organic Seekers) directly from the catalog screen.
