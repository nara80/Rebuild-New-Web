How to Measure Your V-Berth for a Perfect Fitted Sheet

M
MildMate Team
January 15, 2026
· 5 min read
📘 🔗

Measuring a V-Berth is one of the trickiest jobs in marine bedding. Unlike a rectangular mattress, the V-Berth's trapezoidal shape requires four measurements to get a fitted sheet that actually fits. Here's everything you need to know — including which numbers to send us when you order.

What You'll Need

  • A flexible tape measure (at least 200 cm / 80 inches)
  • A pen and paper — or the MildMate measurement form
  • A helper (makes it much easier)
📐 INLINE IMAGE: 1200 × 675 px
📷 In-Post Image Required: 1200 × 675 px (16:9) · JPG/WebP · max 300 KB e.g. photo of measuring tape on V-Berth mattress
Measuring the Head Width (bow end) of a V-Berth. The tape should run across the mattress surface, not along the hull.

The Four Measurements You Need

  • Head Width (Bow): Narrow end at the front of the boat, measured at mattress level
  • Foot Width (Cabin Entry): Wide end where you slide in, measured at mattress level
  • Length: The long dimension from bow to stern — measure along the center line
  • Depth: Mattress thickness for the elastic pocket
"Measure the mattress itself — not the berth opening. The mattress sits inside the hull, so hull measurements are always larger."

Once you have all four measurements, enter them into our V-Berth configurator on any product page to get an instant price. Our team will cut the sheet precisely to your dimensions with a full elastic pocket.

Ordering Your Sheet

Go to any V-Berth product, switch the configurator to V-Berth Boat Sheet mode, enter your four measurements, choose your fabric, and add to cart. We'll send a measurement confirmation email within 1 business day before we start cutting.
