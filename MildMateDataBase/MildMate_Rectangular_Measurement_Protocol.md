# Knowledge Base: MildMate Rectangular Mattress Measurement Protocol

## Service Overview
This protocol adapts the **"Zero-Friction"** workflow from specialized marine bedding to standard home and commercial mattresses. It is designed for **Rectangular Prism ($W \times L \times D$)** geometries to ensure a high-precision technical fit for non-marine environments.

---

## The Value Proposition: "The Hybrid Zero-Friction Method"
Measuring mattresses for custom protectors or high-tension fitted sheets can be error-prone. MildMate eliminates this friction by leveraging engineering data and offering flexible verification methods.

- **Research-First Strategy:** For standard mattress brands and sizes (e.g., Tempur-Pedic, Sealy, IKEA), MildMate identifies the standard dimensions first. The customer is asked to "verify" rather than "measure from scratch" [cite: 101, 102].
- **Dual-Verification Choice:** - **Tape Measure:** Traditional centimeter-to-centimeter verification against the provided blueprint [cite: 104, 170].
    - **Credit Card Scaling:** Standard 8.56 cm (3.375 in) "flips" for customers without tools or those seeking maximum precision [cite: 91, 171].
- **Pricing Logic:** Pricing is estimated based on surface area ($W \times L$) and volume/depth ($D$) for specific product categories [cite: 137].

## The 7-Step Workflow for Rectangular Products

### 1. Model Identification & Research
The customer provides their mattress brand/model or standard size. MildMate researches the engineering specifications for Width ($W$), Length ($L$), and Depth ($D$) [cite: 130, 131].

### 2. Pre-Filled Template Delivery
MildMate sends a digital blueprint in **Vibrant Blue (#2c96f4)** pre-populated with standard dimensions through the consultation channel [cite: 107, 113, 132].

### 3. Verification (Tape or Card)
The customer confirms the pre-filled dimensions using their preferred tool:
- **Tape Measure:** Checking physical CM measurements against the template [cite: 135].
- **Credit Card Method:** Using any standard card (exactly 8.56 cm long) to provide "card counts" for all three axes ($W$, $L$, and $D$) [cite: 136].

### 4. Submission & Refinement
The customer confirms the dimensions or provides minor adjustments.

### 5. Final Digital Drafting
MildMate refines the blueprint with technical markings, ensuring it features the **MildMate logo** and specific model name [cite: 113].

### 6. Final Quote & Approval
MildMate provides a "down-to-the-penny" price quote based on the chosen product type [cite: 109, 137]:
- **Fitted Sheet:** CloudSoft or BreezePlus with 360° elastic anchor [cite: 138, 139].
- **Waterproof Protector:** Performance TPU (Breathable/No-Crinkle) [cite: 139, 140].
- **6-Side Encasement:** Zippered Performance TPU for full coverage [cite: 140, 141].

### 7. Physical Order Conversion
Upon approval, the customer proceeds to the physical order, often utilizing a service-fee credit if applicable [cite: 105, 110].

## Technical Manufacturing Standards (The Golden Formula)

To ensure the signature **"MildMate Cinch-Fit"**, manufacturing follows these parameters [cite: 142]:

- **The Pocket Notch ($N$):** $N = D + 3 \text{ inches}$ (Includes 2" tuck-under and 1" sewing allowance) [cite: 143].
- **Tolerance:** 1% - 2% size tolerance for a professional, taut finish [cite: 144].
- **Hardware:** Fitted products feature a **360° heavy-duty elastic anchor** [cite: 111, 139].
